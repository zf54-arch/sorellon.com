Health and Safety Statement

This Health and Safety Statement outlines Sorellon Limited’s approach to managing health and safety in accordance with applicable UK legislation and recognised good practice.

1. Policy Statement

Sorellon Limited is committed to ensuring, so far as is reasonably practicable, the health, safety, and welfare of those affected by its business activities.

2. Legal Compliance

The company seeks to comply with all relevant health and safety legislation, including the Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 and associated regulations.

3. Scope

This statement applies to Sorellon Limited’s business activities and to persons who may be affected by those activities, including employees, contractors, and visitors where applicable.

4. Risk Management

Health and safety risks are assessed on a proportionate basis having regard to the nature of the business. Appropriate controls are implemented where reasonably practicable.

5. Responsibilities

Overall responsibility for health and safety rests with the management of Sorellon Limited. Day to day responsibilities are assigned as appropriate to the scale of operations.

6. Training and Information

Information, instruction, and training are provided where appropriate to ensure that individuals understand relevant health and safety requirements.

7. Monitoring and Review

Health and safety arrangements are kept under review and updated as necessary to reflect changes in legislation, guidance, or business activities.

8. Legal Notice

This statement is provided for information and compliance purposes only. It does not constitute a contractual obligation or guarantee.

  • Sorellon Limited makes no representations regarding specific risk outcomes.
  • Implementation of controls is subject to practicality and proportionality.
  • To the maximum extent permitted by law, Sorellon Limited accepts no liability for loss arising from reliance on this statement.

Nothing in this statement excludes liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence.
